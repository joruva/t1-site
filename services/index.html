Capabilities

The full arc of building a physical product business.

Six areas where decades of operating experience — not theory — change the outcome. Engagements are scoped to where T1 can move the needle, from a single decision to a full product program.

01 — Develop

New Product Development

Taking a product from concept to a launch that actually ships — with the stage-gate discipline that keeps programs honest and the connected-product know-how that most teams learn the hard way.

  • Product roadmap, requirements, and stage-gate governance
  • Connected, smart-home, and voice / AVS-integrated product architecture
  • Certification and launch-readiness planning (UL and equivalent)
  • Cross-functional program leadership through to first shipment
02 — Connect

Connected Products & Software

Connected, app-driven, voice-enabled products — and the embedded, cloud, and mobile software behind them. Real experience turning a legacy hardware line into a true IoT platform, from architecture through launch.

  • Connected-product and IoT architecture — Wi-Fi, Thread / Matter, and cloud
  • Companion mobile apps, cloud services, and over-the-air software strategy
  • Voice and ecosystem integration (Amazon Alexa, Apple Home / AirPlay)
  • Build-vs-buy for software and connectivity, including acquisition
03 — Make

Manufacturing & Sourcing

The realities of getting a product built well, at cost, at volume — across a North American and offshore manufacturing footprint run at national scale.

  • BOM and cost engineering; design-to-cost targets
  • Contract-manufacturer and supplier selection, negotiation, and management
  • Production ramp, quality systems, and supply-chain resilience
  • Domestic + offshore footprint strategy and tradeoffs
04 — Win

Category & Channel Strategy

How a product becomes a category leader — through the right channels, the right national-account relationships, and positioning that holds up against entrenched incumbents.

  • Channel strategy across mass retail and specifier / pro channels
  • National-account and distribution agreement strategy
  • Category positioning, line architecture, and pricing
  • Competitive strategy and share-growth planning
05 — Comply

Regulatory & Standards Strategy

Turning codes, standards, and certification requirements from a cost of doing business into a source of advantage — especially in safety-regulated product categories where the rules shape the market.

  • Standards-response strategy (e.g. UL standard revisions) and product roadmaps to meet them
  • Codes, mandates, and regulatory landscape as demand drivers
  • Certification pathway planning across US and international bodies
  • Government-affairs and industry-body engagement strategy
06 — Transact

M&A & Diligence Advisory

Product and technology judgment for deals — knowing what a product portfolio is really worth, where the integration risk hides, and how a business gets carved out or sold. Informed by acquisitions, integrations, and a divestiture led from the inside.

  • Buy-side product, technology, and operations diligence
  • Post-acquisition integration planning and execution
  • Carve-out and divestiture readiness
  • Build-vs-buy and portfolio strategy
